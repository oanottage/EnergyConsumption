Energy Consumption Prediction Project


I am Onassis Nottage, a Master's student in Big Data and Artificial Intelligence at the University of Barcelona. I have a dual degree in Engineering Physics and Mathematics from the University of The Bahamas. Currently, I work as a Business Intelligence Analyst at Cable Bahamas Ltd. and have experience working as an Assistant Product Specialist and Analyst at Bahamas Telecommunications Company Ltd.


My motivation for this project is to leverage my skills in data science and artificial intelligence to predict energy consumption accurately. This project aims to help people make informed decisions about their energy usage and assist the energy industry in planning for the future. For this project, I plan to use data from the US Energy Information Administration and NOAA to analyze and predict energy consumption based on various features such as temperature, humidity, and time of day.


My goal for this project is to create an accurate prediction model that can help the energy industry better plan for future energy demand. This project can also help individuals understand and manage their energy consumption more efficiently. I will use tools such as Python, Flask, Pandas, Numpy, and Scikit-learn to create the dashboard and prediction model.


About

The goal of this project is to predict energy consumption based on various features such as temperature, humidity, time of day, etc. The two sources of data for this project could be energy consumption data and weather data. You can get the energy consumption data from the US Energy Information Administration (https://www.eia.gov/opendata/qb.php?category=2122628) and the weather data from NOAA (https://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/cdo-web/webservices/v2).


Dashboard Features

  • Energy consumption data visualization
  • Weather data visualization
  • Prediction model output visualization
  • User input form for data selection and prediction
